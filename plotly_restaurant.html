NYC Restaurant Inspection Data in Plotly

Let’s take a look at data from NYC restaurant inspections. This data set contains information about the results of food safety inspections conducted by the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene. It was taken from the DOHMH via NYC Open Data in October 2017. To learn more, please visit the data set’s host website, where additional links to the current raw data and a data dictionary can also be found.

Due to the size of the dataset (397584 observations), let’s just look at restaurants with critical violations in Manhattan. First, an essential cuisine in New York: pizza.

Pizza Plot

pizza_df = 
  rest_inspec %>% 
  filter(
    boro == "MANHATTAN", 
    critical_flag == "Critical",
    cuisine_description == c("Pizza", "Pizza/Italian")
  ) %>% 
  select(-c(camis, phone, record_date, inspection_date))
